(1)-(2) Immediate from antisymmetry of subtraction and definition of Bregman divergence.

(3) Expand the Bregman divergence (Definition \ref{def:bregman}) to third order in powers of $h$ and $k$:
\begin{align}
D[\phi_0 + h \| \phi_0 + k] &= \int [V(|\phi_0+h|^2) - V(|\phi_0+k|^2) - V'(|\phi_0+k|^2)(|\phi_0+h|^2 - |\phi_0+k|^2)] d\mu.
\end{align}

Expanding $|\phi_0 + h|^2 = |\phi_0|^2 + 2\text{Re}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot h) + |h|^2$:
\begin{align}
V(|\phi_0 + h|^2) &= V(|\phi_0|^2) + V'(|\phi_0|^2)[2\text{Re}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot h) + |h|^2] \\
&\quad + \frac{V''(|\phi_0|^2)}{2}[2\text{Re}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot h)]^2 + V'''(|\phi_0|^2)[2\text{Re}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot h)]^3 + \cdots
\end{align}

Computing the divergence and taking the antisymmetrized combination cancels even-order terms, leaving only odd-order terms:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{A}[\phi_0+h, \phi_0+k] &= 2V'''(|\phi_0|^2) \int \text{Im}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot h) \text{Im}(\overline{\phi_0} \cdot k) |\phi_0|^2 d\mu + O(4).
\end{align}

(4) Non-degeneracy follows from strict convexity of $V$ (condition V2 in Axiom \ref{ax:configSpace}), ensuring $V''' \neq 0$ in generic regions.
