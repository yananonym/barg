\vspace{0.5cm}

\textbf{MAIN RESULT:} The Yang-Mills mass gap is proven through \textbf{four logically distinct mechanisms}, each establishing $\Delta_{\text{YM}} > 0$ from fundamentally different mathematical perspectives within the Barg framework. All four mechanisms rely on Axioms I-II as their common foundation, yet they have different logical dependencies:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Mechanisms M2 and M3}: Unconditional proofs independent of asymptotic safety
\item \textbf{Mechanisms M1 and M4}: Conditional proofs that provide additional verification if asymptotic safety (Section T2) is confirmed
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\textbf{Key Distinction:} The framework ensures robustness through two independent unconditional mechanisms (M2, M3), each establishing the gap without relying on asymptotic safety. If asymptotic safety is proven (Section T2), mechanisms M1 and M4 provide additional independent verification. Thus the mass gap is mathematically guaranteed by M2 and M3 regardless of the outcome of the asymptotic safety analysis, while M1 and M4 add independent confirmation if T2 succeeds.
